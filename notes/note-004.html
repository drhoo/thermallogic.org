Research Note 004 · 研究札记

Maxwell: Flow, Accumulation, and Dissipation流动、积累与耗散。

A hotspot is not merely a high temperature. It is a local imbalance between heat entering, spreading, storing, and escaping.

热点不只是高温，而是热的进入、扩散、储存和逸出之间的局部失衡。

Maxwell’s Theory of Heat gives us a simple but powerful operating rule: if heat enters or is generated within a region faster than it can leave, that region warms. If removal equals supply, the thermal state may remain steady even while heat continues to flow.

麦克斯韦的热理论给出一个简单但有力的操作规则：如果热进入或产生的速度快于逸出的速度，该区域就会升温。如果移除量等于输入量，即使热仍在流动，热状态也可以保持稳定。

This matters because a hotspot is not just a temperature reading. It is a failure of balance: generation, pathway, storage, boundary, or shedding has become insufficient for the load.

这很重要，因为热点不只是温度读数，而是一种平衡失效：产生、路径、储存、边界或散出中的某一环节无法承受负载。

Dynamic Thermal Control™ therefore begins by asking what kind of thermal failure is present: is heat being generated too quickly, trapped locally, blocked at an interface, stored without release, or shed through the wrong boundary?

因此，动态热控制首先要问：当前是哪一种热失效？热是否产生过快，被局部困住，被界面阻挡，被储存而未释放，或通过错误的边界散出？

To control heat, first identify the failure mode.

要控制热，首先要识别失效模式。
